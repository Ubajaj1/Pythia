Netflix Password Sharing Crackdown — Internal Decision Memo
============================================================
Date: April 2023
Author: Netflix Strategy Team
Classification: Internal — Decision Support

BACKGROUND
----------
Netflix estimates that approximately 100 million households share passwords
outside the subscriber's primary household. This represents roughly 43% of
Netflix's 230 million paid subscribers as of Q1 2023.

Co-CEO Ted Sarandos and CFO Spence Neumann have publicly committed to
ending password sharing. Content chief Bela Bajaria supports the position.
Product leadership, led by Greg Peters, has designed the "extra member"
feature priced at $7.99/month in the US for shared accounts.

Early tests in Latin America (Peru, Chile, Costa Rica, beginning March 2022)
showed initial subscriber drops of 4-7%, followed by recovery within two
quarters as affected users either subscribed, added extra members, or
churned permanently. Net result across Latin America test markets after
12 months: +3.5% subscriber growth, +12% revenue growth.

CURRENT POSITIONS WITHIN NETFLIX
-------------------------------
SUPPORTIVE:
- Ted Sarandos (Co-CEO): "This is a $2B+ annual opportunity"
- Spence Neumann (CFO): Projects 7-10% revenue uplift by year-end 2023
- Product & engineering: Infrastructure ready for global rollout May 2023

CAUTIOUS:
- Regional marketing teams (US, UK): Fear severe backlash given existing
  account-based user habits of 10+ years
- Customer support leadership: Anticipates 3-5x ticket volume spike
- PR team: Notes significant negative press coverage already forming

EXTERNAL ANALYST POSITIONS (as of April 2023)
---------------------------------------------
- Morgan Stanley (Benjamin Swinburne): "Neutral. Execution risk high."
- Wells Fargo (Steven Cahall): "Overweight. The Latam data is convincing."
- MoffettNathanson (Michael Nathanson): "Underperform. US users more price-
  sensitive than Latam, will churn."
- Bank of America (Jessica Reif Ehrlich): "Buy. Short-term pain, long-term
  gain. Stock target $410."
- Pivotal Research (Jeffrey Wlodarczak): "Hold. Cannot predict US reaction
  given password sharing is deeply embedded in user behavior."

KEY METRICS
-----------
- Netflix Q1 2023 subscribers: 232.5 million
- Q1 2023 revenue: $8.16 billion
- Netflix Q1 2023 stock price: $325 (trading range $290-$345)
- Password sharing households (estimated): 100 million
- Latam test conversion rate (paid extra member or new sub): 41% of affected
- Latam test permanent churn rate: 8% of affected users

RISKS ENUMERATED
---------------
1. Brand damage — 10-year tolerance of sharing is now a de facto feature
2. Competitor opportunism — Disney+, Max ready with "we don't do this" campaigns
3. Subscriber cliff — worst case 15-20M subscriber loss in H2 2023
4. Class action lawsuits possible around account terms-of-service changes
5. Regulatory scrutiny in EU around account control provisions

TIMELINE
--------
- March 2022: Latin America pilot begins
- October 2022: Extra Member feature launches in pilot countries
- April 2023: Final go/no-go decision for global rollout
- May 23, 2023: Targeted global launch date
- Q3 2023: First full quarter to measure impact
